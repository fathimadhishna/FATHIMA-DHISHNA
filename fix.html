Comparison of Electrochemical oxidation and ozonation for the degradation of Propylparaben

Click to turn page

Abstract

Thesis

This thesis is a comparitive study of two Advanced oxidation process technologies,Electrochemical oxidation and Ozonation for the degradation of an emerging contaminant Propylparaben in water.

Major objectives:

  • To degrade Propylparaben in water using Electrochemical oxidation and Ozonation process
  • To assess graphite sheet as an electrode material for Electrochemical oxidation process
  • To optimize the variables such as pH and treatment time for electrochemical oxidation process
  • To compare the efficiencies of Electrochemical degradation and ozonation at optimal conditions
  • Environmental techniques used:
COD UV Visible spectroscopy

03

Source to sink: A possible mechanism of transport of Mangiferin in Salacia brunoniana

and potentiality of leaf extract in anti-cancerous studies

Click to turn page

Abstract

Thesis

This project investigates the phyto chemistry and pharmacological potential of Salacia brunoniana focusing on the source to sink transport of mangiferin and evaluating its presence in leaves as an alternative to root extraction.

Major objectives:

  • To investigate the phytochemistry of leaf
  • To compare anti-diabetic properties of root and leaf
  • To advice an effective remedy against over exploitation of root
  • To test the cytotoxicity of leaf extract and suggest its potential in anti-cancerous studies
  • Analytical techniques used:
HPLC Cytotoxicity Methanolic extraction

This study analyses the uses of X-ray flourescence spectroscopy for the analysis of soil samples from Kabini river basin.
